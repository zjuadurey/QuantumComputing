## Recommended Insertion for Response Item 2

To directly address the reviewer’s concern, we added a structured multi-component stress test that makes the coupling graph progressively denser in a controlled way. Concretely, we consider a deterministic family of potentials obtained by adding Fourier components one by one, and we report two controls: (i) a fixed-total-coupling setting, which isolates graph density from total interaction strength, and (ii) a fixed-per-component setting, which captures the regime where both graph density and total coupling grow.

In the fixed-total-coupling experiment (`\alpha_{\mathrm{tot}} = 0.5`, `K_0 = 5`, `t = 0.5`, `R_hops = 1`), increasing the number of Fourier components from `J = 1` to `J = 8` enlarges the reference set from `|R| = 3` to `|R| = 17`, and the reduced dictionary size from `|K||R| = 243` to `1377`. At the same time, the density error stays near `3 \times 10^{-2}`, while `\|\Delta Z\|_F` remains in the `10^{-3}` to `10^{-2}` range and the a priori leakage stays explicitly computable throughout. This shows that denser coupling graphs do increase the reduced representation size, but do not automatically destroy approximation quality in the tested regime.

To separate graph density from coupling strength, we also ran a fixed-per-component control (`\alpha_{\mathrm{each}} = 0.1`). In that setting, increasing `J` again grows `|R|` monotonically from `3` to `17` and `|K||R|` from `243` to `1377`, while the leakage increases from `4.84 \times 10^{-2}` to `1.62 \times 10^{-1}`. This makes the regime boundary more explicit: as the Hamiltonian becomes both denser and stronger, the certified reduced model remains usable, but the dimensional advantage shrinks and the leakage proxy rises.

We also quantified the closure-depth tradeoff on the densest structured case (`J = 8`). Under fixed total coupling, increasing the BFS closure depth from `R_hops = 1` to `3` grows `|R|` from `17` to `115` and `|K||R|` from `1377` to `9315`, while reducing `\|\Delta Z\|_F` from `3.57 \times 10^{-3}` to `4.78 \times 10^{-4}` and lowering the leakage proxy from `1.01 \times 10^{-1}` to `7.77 \times 10^{-2}`. This directly quantifies the cost-accuracy tradeoff requested by the reviewer: denser coupling and deeper closure do increase the reduced dimension, but the effect is transparent, measurable, and accompanied by improved operator-level accuracy.

Finally, these additional stress tests also clarify that in this regime the reported density error is largely dominated by the task cutoff `K_0`, not by the shadow dictionary approximation itself: across all generated rebuttal stress tests, the extra shadow contribution to density error relative to the low-pass baseline never exceeds `4.2 \times 10^{-4}` in absolute value (below `1.5%` of `\varepsilon_\rho`). For that reason, `\|\Delta Z\|_F` and `\ell_{\mathrm{rms}}` are the cleaner diagnostics of shadow-model quality in the complexity stress test.

## Optional Short Version

To address the reviewer’s concern about overly simple cosine potentials, we added a structured multi-component stress test that progressively densifies the coupling graph. Under a fixed-total-coupling control (`\alpha_{\mathrm{tot}} = 0.5`, `K_0 = 5`, `t = 0.5`), increasing the number of Fourier components from `J = 1` to `J = 8` grows the reference set from `|R| = 3` to `17` and the reduced dictionary size from `243` to `1377`, while keeping the density error near `3 \times 10^{-2}` and the operator error `\|\Delta Z\|_F` in the `10^{-3}` to `10^{-2}` range. We further quantified the closure-depth tradeoff on the densest case (`J = 8`): increasing `R_hops` from `1` to `3` grows `|R|` from `17` to `115`, but reduces `\|\Delta Z\|_F` from `3.57 \times 10^{-3}` to `4.78 \times 10^{-4}` and lowers the leakage proxy from `1.01 \times 10^{-1}` to `7.77 \times 10^{-2}`. These results make the regime boundary more explicit: denser coupling graphs do reduce the dimensional advantage by enlarging `R`, but the tradeoff is transparent, measurable, and remains compatible with a meaningful reduced representation in the tested regime.
